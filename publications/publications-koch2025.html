Explainable AI species identifications using Vision Language Models

    Abstract

    Advances in computer vision provide us with much needed species classifications at scale. These classifications remain a black box, however: unlike human experts, AI models lack the domain knowledge to explain their findings. By providing existing, general purpose Vision Language Models with domain knowledge in the form of pictures and descriptions from identification keys, we let them assess whether or not diagnostic traits are visible in an image. We investigated if this provides insights that can explain and/or improve species classifications, and found that this method is especially suited to generate meaningful, educational text explainations for classifications.
